Employment Opportunities

Overview

As mentioned in the working styles section, there are many people who have lost their jobs as it was found that robots and machinery can perform the work more efficiently and save the business on wage expenses. The two following accordian tabs will look at jobs that have been created as well as jobs that have been lost as a result of the introduction of ICT.

Drawbacks - Areas Where Jobs Have Been Lost

Manual Repetitive JobsFactory manufacturing, spraying, welding, packing andassembling have seen robots replacing humans. Robots are replacing people because they can do the same job much faster, they can work 24/7 and they do not require wages.

Shop Work
Online shopping (like Amazon) has caused a reduction in the number of shops found on our high streets. More and more people are shopping online because is it generally cheaper and they can get goods delivered. Hence, the demand for shop staff has also decreased.

Bank Cashiers
The use of ATMs (Automated Teller Machines) have reduced the need for bank cashiers.

Office Work
Traditional office work such as typists and filers have been replaced by computers.
One person who knows how to use Word Processors and Databases can replace
many typists and filers.

Telephone Exchange
Telephone operators have been replaced by computer systems that make telephone connections automatically.

Library Work
With the launch of e-books (electronic books) the future of traditional libraries could be at risk. People could easily download an electronic book instead of having to visit a library.


Benefits - Areas Where Jobs Have Been Created

Network Managers/ Technicians
These jobs have been created due to the need for bigcomputer networks within companies. Network managers make sure that computers on the network run smoothly and are secure.

Web Design
The Internet has seen a huge demand for bothcommercial (business) and personal web sites. Web designers create these sites and keep them up to date.

Systems Analyst
These workers are responsible for analysing current systems and accordingly designing, developing, implementing, documenting and evaluating a new one.

Programmers
Programmers create all the software that can be used on a computer. This is a huge industry and produces things like operating systems, applications (word, excel etc) and computer games.

Computer Engineers
Engineers are needed to build the high-tech computers that we use today.

Personal Experience

A cousin of mine who is employed as a server engineer at a telecommunications company explained to me that the company had made several telephone operators redundant and replaced them with Engineers who would build the computer systems to perform the job that the operators used to perform.
