Life in the Information Age - Conclusion

To summarise, Working Styles have been significantly impacted after the introduction of ICT. Peoplehave begun working from home, abroad and have the ability to access files and data. They can also communicate with colleagues outside of the office. This has increased business' productivity and have made them more efficient and cost effective. However many workers do not approve of the change in working styles because it means that their employeers are always able to contact them even outside working hours through email or SMS. Mentioned below are some of the scope and limitations of Life in the Information Age.

Scope:

It can be argued that the main purpose of technology is to make life for humans easier. In addition to the benefits that the
internet has already given us [see online services - conclusion], the introduction of technology has make working life easier
for professionals. As mentioned earlier, my mother has the luxury of being able to work from home and respond to students' enquiries through VLE Platforms such as Google Classroom and Edmodo. Having slightly more laid-back work ethics will allow workers to increase their leisure time. Through the internet, users are able to educate themselves on present issues that the world is facing and make a difference by donating online to a charity or NGO.

Limitations:

Despite having created jobs, technology has also replaced many others. Jobs that invlolve manual repetitive work have been replaced with machinery as they are more efficient and cost-effective. An issue with technology such as cellphones, wireless routers and laptops that often goes unnoticed is the radiation that it emits. It is scientifically proven to have negative health effects and can even increase the risk of cancer! Furthermore, in general the use of technology has reduced human effort and this can create a sharp decline in fitness levels in the near future.
