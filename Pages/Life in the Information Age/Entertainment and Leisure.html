Entertainment and Leisure

  • Effects of ICT on Leisure Time
  • Effects of ICT on the Need to Leave Home
  • Benefits and Drawbacks

Microprocessor-controlled devices allow us to do our daily tasks quicker. This gives us more time for leisure and other things that we might enjoy.

Working on the Move

Laptops, PDA's, Mobile Phones, IPads etc, allow us to work on the move which means we get to enjoy more free time. For example, a person could complete my work for the evening using a laptop on the train. This means I don't have to do it when I get home.

More Time Spent Gaming

There has been a huge increase in the demand for gaming over the last 20 years. More and more people spend a large amount of free time playing the latest computer games.

Increased Leisure Time

Microprocessors in household appliances can make sure that the task is completed without you actually being there.
Once you have started the appliance you can go off and do something else and return to it later.

Fitness

Because microprocessor-controlled devices do most of the work for us it means that we are not doing as much hard manual work as we used to.

For example, before washing machines existed, doing the laundry was actually quite a demanding task. You had to wash the clothes by hand and then hang them on a washing line. Now all we need to do it put them in the machine and press 'go'.

However, since microprocessor-controlled devices can reduce the amount of time it takes us to complete our every-day tasks we are left with more free time to do the things we want to do.
Some people choose to spend some of this extra time in the gym getting fit.

Personal Experience

My father's job requires him to travel very often. In the past, when he would travel, he would have to record his favourite TV shows on cassette while he was away so that he could watch them when he got back. However, now with streaming services such as Netflix and Hulu, my father won't need to miss an episode. His subscription to Netflix allows him to view the latest episodes of a variety of TV Shows on their day of release!

Online (E-Commerce):
Goods (of any kind) can be ordered over the Internet and delivered to your door. Any device that allows you to connect to the Internet can be used to shop online. Retailers such as Supermarkets, Entertainment businesses (DVD, Music, Games) and Clothing.

Teleworking (working from home):
As discussed earlier, many people work from home and use ICT to perform tasks and contact clients and colleagues. As long as a person has a computer with an internet connection, email facilities and videoconferencing software.

Online Banking:
Banking can now be carried out online. We can perform tasks such as transferring money, viewing statements, paying bills and applying for loans without actually visiting the bank.

Research:
Rather than going to the library, Research can now be carried out using a computer with an Internet connection. Furthermore information such as bus timetables, shop and movie timings can all be discovered and booked online.

Negative effects of not having to leave the house

Elderly or disabled people can still carry out day-to-day tasks such as shopping and paying bills.

You don't actually meet people face to face as much. This is known as 'social-isolation'.

You can find entertainment even if you cannot go outside due to bad weather.

Not leaving the house is bad for you - lack of exercise etc.

You can carry out day-to-day tasks even if you are sick.
