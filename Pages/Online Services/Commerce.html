What it is

Online Shopping is a method of buying and selling products and services over the internet.
The concept of online shopping was first developed in 1979 by British entrepreneur Michael Aldrich who is credited for being the first person to launch a website that could enable online transaction processing between consumers and businesses.

Online Shopping become popular during the late 1990s when websites like Amazon were established. E commerce is the process consumers go through to purchase products or services over the internet.


More Consumers Prefer Online Shopping
Seventy percent of more than 3,000 online shoppers surveyed in February say they prefer to shop from their favorite retailer online. Half the smartphone owners in the group and nearly 60% of the tablet owners use those devices to make purchases

 

Target Audience

While online shopping has been targeted at everyone with an internet connection, it can be especially useful for the elderly and the disabled who can easily have their items delivered to their doorstep.

In a survey conducted by The Nielsen Company, it was found discovered that electronics are the most commonly purchased items online, followed by cars and software applications.

Fitness for Purpose

Online shopping aims to save customers' time, money and make it more convenient for them. In this regard, online shopping is very successful as consumers do not have to travel, pay gas money or spend time walking through shops. With a simple payment online, the goods are delivered directly to your doorstep.

Non-Internet Alternatives

The most obvious alternative to shopping online is to simply go out to an actual store and purchase your goods over the counter. However, there are other ways to shop without having to leave the house which do not involve usage of the internet.

For example, orders can be placed over the telephone and then have them delivered to your doorstep. This would save customers time and money but this would not be suitable for all products as the purchase of some products require a visual image to be present in order for the customer to make up their mind.

Furthermore, some retail outlets offer catalog orders which allow customers to tick off the products they want to purchase and then mail it to the store. The major issue with catalogs are that they may not feature all the products that the store has to offer.

Aditionally they could tell the customer what measures they need to take before starting to shop online. This may include a guide to anti-virus software, firewall settings and strong password options.

What it is

Online banking is an electronic payment system that allows the customers of a bank to view documents and conduct financial transactions such as withdrawing money, depositing money and paying bills on a website operated by the institution.

Online banking was first introduced in the early 1980s in New York after four major banks: Citibank, Chase Manhattan, Chemical and Manufacturers Hanover started offering home banking services. However this attempt was initially unsuccessful because the videotex system used to provide the service was a commercial failure. Contrastingly, Online Banking became a hit in the UK almost immediately after The Bank of Scotland set up the Homelink system in 1983. This allowed customers to view statements, bank transfers and bill payments online.

Target Audience

Online banking is targeted at those bank customers who do not have to go to the bank to make transactions. Businessmen are good examples as they need to constantly check if their business money transfers are sent or received. People who travel frequently also have the habit of checking their balance while on the move.
