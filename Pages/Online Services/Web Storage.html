Web Storage

  • Google Drive

What it is

Google Drive is a web storage platform that gives users the opportuinty to use applications and store them online on their email account.

Rather than having to purchase expensive word processing, presentation and spreadsheet software, Google drive provides all these programs for free over the internet. Users have the opportunity to use any device with an internet connection to ceate and edit documents on various office suite programs. These documents are then stored online through the users google mail account and can be accessed though any other device the user is logged-in on.


Target Audience

Working professionals are often asked to create reports on their tasks at hand. These people are able
to make use of Google Drive, courtesy of its Office Suite Applications.

Furthermore, Students such as myself often need to write essays and prepare slide shows for assignments and are able to use Google Drive's free applications to create and store home work.

Benefits and Drawbacks
Benefits Drawbacks
Google Drive is absolutely free and anyone with a gmail account can use it. Documents cannot be accessed while offline
All documents are saved online and can be accessed through any device with an internet connection Google has a worldwide license to use, host, store, reproduce, modify and share your documents.
If the computer's hard drive crashes, documents will not be lost Google Drive does not offer features as advanced as some of its Office Suite competitiors.


Fitness for Purpose

Google Drive is fit for purpose because, although it may not have the advanced features that some of its competitors do, it provides the core functions of an Office Suite Program online and for free.

Non-Internet Alternatives

Without the use of the internet, User would need to use an Office Suite Applications package such as Microsoft Office. However, most of these are very expensive to purchase and those that are free are generally of inferior quality.

Alternatively, you could scrap technology as a whole and resort to pen and paper to complete assignments. However, this would be time consuming and the lack of multimedia inegration could make presentations boring.

Possible Enhancements

A major enhancement that can be made to Google Drive is to make its documents downloadable. This would give users a greater incentive to use google drive as they would have the comfort of knowing that they can view their files even when there is no internet connection.

Furthermore, Google could integrate online learning with google drive by having online video tutorials that teach users with no office suite experience how to use Google Drive.

What it is

Dropbox is a cloud storage website that works similar to google drive but is not limited to Office Suite Documents. Dropbox provides storage space for photographs, sound clips, videos, documents, etc. Essentially, Dropbox is like a removable storage device that only works when there is an internet connection.

Dropbox also allows user to share files with each other. In order to let others download a file, a url can be sent to the desired people who can then view the file and download it.

Target Audience

The Target audience for drop box are people who use up a lot of memory space and frequently need to send large files to each other. Additionally, it could also be used by people who prefer having all of their data online rather than on an external hard drive which can be misplaced or stolen.

Benefits and Drawbacks

Benefits

Drawbacks
Very secure as Dropbox uses high-end encryption software to protect the files. If the site is hacked, files may be at danger of being lost.
Dropbox can now be accessed through mobile. Sometimes, locating a file can be time consuming.
With files online rather than on external storage devices, people won't forget or misplace important data. The site cannot be accessed without an internet connection.
Fitness for Purpose

Dropbox is fit for purpose to the extent that it does its job of storing and sharing files online well enough to make it the best cloud storage platform online. However, with slow download rates, no compression options and difficulty in location files, Dropbox definiteley has room for improvement.



Non-Internet Alternatives

If there was no internet, users could still save work on external storage devices such as USBs, Optical Discs, Flash Drives and External Hard Drives. However, sharing this information would be problem as the storage device would need to physically change hands in order to transfer data.

Possible Enhancements

Many users find it difficult to keep their files under the size limit. Dropbox should integrate compression software into their website that allows users to make their files smaller without dramatically dropping the quality.
