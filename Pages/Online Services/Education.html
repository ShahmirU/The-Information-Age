What it is

Online learning applications enable individuals to acquire new skills through the use of the internet. Online learning can be self-paced, which means that user can chose whenever they would like to learn, or instructor led, where there are specific timings for sessions which users must virtually attend. In recent years, online graduate and undergraduate degrees have become very popular as it caters to people who must balance work/study life.

 

Target Audience

Education has no age limits! Therefore, online learning is aimed at everyone looking to acquire knowledge and new skills. The material that users are learning may however vary depending on age group.
As mentioned in the upper tab, people with jobs can make use of online degrees as they can learn whilst at home, thus not affecting their efficiency at work.

- Online University Student


Benefits and Drawbacks

Benefits

Drawbacks

Can allow the student to learn without hindering their personal lives too much.

Connections may be slow.

Workers can learn during their free time as opposed to actually attending a class which may clash with their working hours.

Students who like learning kinesthetically may be unsatisfied as they will not have a hands-on experience

Reduces gas bills as there is no need to travel to school.

The professor/instructor may not be available to tend to queries at all times.
Fitness for Purpose

Online learning is fit for purpose because it reaches the people who work and do not have time to physically attend classes or the people who only want to enhance their knowledge (e.g. become proficien in a new program) and do not need to be enrolled in a univerity to do so.

Non-Internet Alternatives

As mentioned in the ‘what it is’ tab, there are two types of online learning. Instructor led and self-paced.
Instructor led learning cannot be done without an internet connection unless the student is to physically attend a lecture or sit in a classroom, which may not be suitable for everyone.

With self-paced courses, it is up to the student as to how much they would like to cover at a time. Therefore, self-paced courses can be loaded onto a CD-ROM, USB Drive or audio tape.

Possible Enhancements

A lot of students struggle to keep up with the professor during lectures. The internet can help solve this problem by having all instructor led classes/lectures recorded so that students can view them later when revising.

Connect all students in the class to a VLE [see next section] where students can share and submit work.

What it is

A Virtual Learning Environment essentially a classroom online. This allows students and teachers to share resources. Many VLEs come equipped with tools that make teachers’ lives easier. This can include automated multiple choice marking, classroom seating, peer to peer assessment and allows them to give direct feedback to students.

VLEs were originally created for distance learning but are now used by all types of educational institutions. VLEs can help students and teachers organize their work as the need for paper is removed.

Target Audience

VLEs can be used by anyone who is enrolled in an institute of education and has basic ICT skills. VLEs they are simply a way of making student’s lives easier. However, primary school students may have not yet developed the skills needed to use VLEs. 

Benefits and Drawbacks

Benefits

Drawbacks

Many VLEs do not require the schools to pay any money when registering.

Students can be easily distracted when using the internet

Students can be more organized as they can store all their documents online rather than using paper.

Training is necessary for Teachers or Professors who do not have advanced enough IT skills to run a VLE.

Students can be notified of any relevant information anytime

Lack of face-to-face interactions will mean problems cannot be resolved immediately.
Fitness for Purpose

Most people would agree that VLEs are fit for purpose only as they are used as an extension to the learning that students receive in school.

VLEs were made to help students find their time in school or college easier and are very successful in doing so by helping students organize time, store resources and stay on top of deadlines. They were not however, made to replace school textbooks entirely.    

Non-Internet Alternatives

There are no realistic alternatives to VLEs that do not involve using the internet. If VLEs are not used, students and teachers will only be able to share resources in person.

Additionally, students will need to use paper-based methods to keep track of homework and timetables.


Possible Enhancements

VLEs should have integrated Video Conferencing that allows students and teachers to get the face-to-face feeling they would in an actual classroom.

VLEs should also have additional topical resources for students who would like to go above and beyond what they learn in class.
