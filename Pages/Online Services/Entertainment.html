What it is

A multi-user game enables gamers from across the world to play the same game at the same time. The internet allows players to simultaneously play talk to others on game chat to make it feel no different to having a friend over at your house.

The first ever gaming console, the Magnavox Odyssey was released in 1972. However, the gaming industry wasn’t popularized until Atari released the arcade game Pong (pictured below).

Target Audience

The gaming audience is split up into two different categories, “Hardcore” and “Casual.” This difference isn’t necessarily created by age as gaming tournaments can consist of  40 year old gamers as well as 10 year old gamers.

Casual gamers just enjoy playing video games as a hobby and to pass time. They aren’t likely to participate in any major tournaments and would only need a console, TV and perhaps an internet access to enjoy gaming.

Hardcore gamers on the other hand have integrated gaming into their lifestyle and many even earn decent money from participating in contests or uploading content to social media. They are likely to buy products such as gaming chairs, headsets, specialized/modified controllers, gaming glassed and any other products that they believe will improve their performance in game.

Benefits and Drawbacks

Benefits

Drawbacks

People use Online gaming and video sharing platforms as a means to interact with people.

Online gaming culture can promote Cyberbullying and violence.

Many video games are intellectual and involve knowledge and strategy to win.

People can become addicted and become a social stigma.

Thanks to the integration of gaming with the internet, jobs such as professional gamers have been created. [see UNILAD article below]

Constantly playing video games can have negative impacts on health such as eye strain. Repetitive Strain Injury and in extreme cases, Deep Vein Thrombosis

Video games have been scientifically proven to
improve hand-eye coordination.

Gaming consoles and other equipment can sometimes be costly.

Fitness for Purpose

The integration of the internet was the biggest step in the evolution of gaming as it allowed gamers from all around the world to play with each other. Online gaming is fit for purpose because it entertains people and allows them to interact with others who share the same interests as them.

Non-Internet Alternatives

Without the internet, players would have to actually go to each other’s houses in order to play a video game together. Alternatively they could simply play single player games and try and beat each other’s high scores. If Video games did not exist at all, people would need to play simpler games to pass time such as board games, puzzles and cars.

Possible Enhancements

In order to prevent children from playing for too long or playing games that exceed their age limit, consoles should have parental blocking and supervision. This would allow parents to monitor the content played and amount of time spent on the console by their children to ensure that they don’t become addicted or influenced by online gaming culture.  

What it is

Today, there are thousands of radio stations around the world. Thanks to the internet, we are able to tune into any radio station of our choice. When the radio was first introduced, its concept was relatively simple. It worked by transmitting signals through frequencies which were later converted into sound. It was mainly used by ships at sea for navigation purposes. Now, with everything being digital, you are simply able to tune into a radio station without the need to scan for frequencies.

Target Audience

Radio stations do not usually target a generic audience. Every radio station has their own target audience. In the United Arab Emirates, VirginRadioDubai is the most common radio station among teenagers and young-adults as it plays new trending music while the older population tend to listen to DubaiEye103.8 for its business talks and regular news updates. Almost all popular radio stations now have live streaming over the internet in order to reach a larger audience.


Benefits and Drawbacks

Benefits

Drawbacks

The majority of radio stations are free to listen to and do not require a subscription fee.

Connections may be slow and can cause the stream to buffer.

You do not need to purchase a radio player if to listen to the radio from home.

 

There is a large variety of stations to choose from.

 
Fitness for Purpose

Online radio is fit for purpose because it gives users the opportunity to listen to their favourite music and talk shows anywhere with an internet connection. This allows users to keep up to date with the news and/or the latest hits!

Non-Internet Alternatives

Without an internet connection, the only way to listen to the radio would be through an FM Radio Device or while sitting in your car. Alternatively, you could buy a smartphone that comes equipped with FM Radio. However, you will find that there is not a very large variety to choose from.


Possible Enhancements

Although there are apps on smartphones that allow you to stream radio stations, there isn’t a large variety. Radio stations should aim to launch their own apps to allow people to listen to their station while on the move.
