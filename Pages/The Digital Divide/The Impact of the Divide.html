The Impact Of The Divide

  • Economic Impact
  • Social Impact
  • Educational Impact
  • Cultural Impact

In the online services – commerce section, I mentioned the numerous advantages to consumers who make use of online shopping and banking.
In addition to consumers, businesses also enjoy many advantages that lead to increased profits. Throught the internet, they have  the ability to set up websites and sell their products and services to people all around the world. Furthermore, by investing in a computer or two, businesses can be more organised by having all their documents on the PC rather than spending money on paper. All this adds to a business’s efficiency and helps them cut costs in the long run.

Due to the increase in Businesses using ICT, jobs have been created as specialists are needed to maintain the technology.



Now let’s look at the reverse. In countries where technology is not as prevelant in business activties, they find it difficult to compete with their counterparts from abroad in terms of efficiency and revenue. Additionally, there are less jobs created. This means that as unemployment rates in developed countries begin to fall, they remain the same in third world countries.This has further worsened the divide.

We discussed that the digital divide has increased the gap between unemployment rates in different countries. Unfortunately, it does not end there. In every country, there is a technological social conflict present because there are people who are wealthy and can afford computers and there are people who cannot. People who do not have internet access are at a serious disadvantage as they are unable to use the internet to keep up with the news, socialise with people online or look for job opportunities.

In the United Kingdom, it is an obligation for every school to have internet access and to teach ICT to their pupils. However, this is not the case is many countries.
Many schools have been pushing parents to get their child a laptop for educational purposes due to the endless resources, informational websites and statistics that it provides students with. According to the Guardian, students who have internet access are more likely to get better grades than students who do not. This means that they will then go on to attend better universities and therefore, have better job prospects.



In addition to an exam advantage, students from around the world with an internet access can get linked together and work on projects while learning about each others cultures.

Although the internet has been a revolutionary platform for free-speech, there are countries that have limited the amount of the internet that a user can navigate and banned websites which they believe opposes the country’s culture, tradition or religion.

Local Examples

In the United Arab Emirates, an Islamic country, the government has banned many websited that features pornography, gambling and other content that conflicts with their religious beliefs.

Global Examples

In North Korea, a strictly communist country, only a handful of government-authorized persons are allowed to use the internet. For the reast of the population, the government launched their own intranet in 2000 called Kwangmyong. However, this network has been accused by the rest of the world for providing false information to its users.
