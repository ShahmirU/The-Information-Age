Factors Affecting The Digital Divide

Infrastructure

Countries around the world vary in terms of communication infrastructure. In many developing countries, people do not have access to telephone lines or a broadband connection.

Level of technology

Wireless technology is becoming increasingly popular and is a concept that should be adopted by developing countries in order to connect their population to the internet. Many developing countries may have some usage of technology in everyday life, but the methods may be old-fashioned and could be outdated in edveloped countries.


Quality of Connection

Educational institutions are increasingly trying to ensure that their students are ‘computer literate’ in order to prepare them for life in the digital age. [See previous section]  However, in order for them to be able to purchase the gadgets, software and network devices necessary, they must be awarded grants by the government of the country in which they operate. Governments in third world countries are unable to do this while others are unable to build the infrastructure that is needed to allow homes and businesses to connect to the internet.

Local Measures

Being a developed country with very large usage of technology, it is the UAE's job to bridge the gap between countries like itself and countries in the middle east with poor infrastructure and low computer literacy rates. The UAE has called for Arab governments to join forces and establish technological centres in the region such as The Dubai Internet City and The Cairo Smart Village. This is done in the hope that it will encourage people to become computer literate and improve the infrastructure of the country.

- Dubai Internet City

With the intention of becoming the centre of the Middle East's IT Industry, HH Sheikh Mohammed (Prime Minister of The UAE) invested 200 million dollars into building the state-of-the-art Dubai Internet City which opened in 2000. The technology park hosts prominent technology companies such as Microsoft, IBM and Cisco. Historically, The UAE's population had never significantly been involved with the innovation or creation of new technology. The Dubai Internet City aims to change that by getting more locals interested in IT so that they pursue careers in the field.

Global Measures

In recent times, the countries in which technology is on the rise have been giving financial aid to third world countries to try and improve their infrastructure. A recent example was India, who gave financial aid to nearly 40 African countries and held a summit for their leaders exhibiting the steps involved in devloping the country from a technological standpoint.

Other countries should continue to take similar initiatives to ensure that countries do not fall back on the advancements in technology being made on a daily basis.

 

Individual Families

Acquiring the network devices and gadgets needed to connect to the internet is not cheap. In addition to the equipment, there is software such as anti-viruses that must be purchased for the internet to run seamlessly.
Families with low incomes spend a larger proportion of their income on basic necessities such as food and water. Therefore, they cannot afford the cost necessary to buy a computer and hence, do not have access the internet.

-

Businesses

Similarly, Businesses need to take into consideration whether purchasing computers and having a network in their office is worth the expense. Since most business’s main motive is profit, they are unlikely to increase their overheads by purchasing a large number of computers as well as other network devices including broadband; unless they believe having access to the internet in their offices will really help their cause.

Local Measures

There is a large Wealth Gap present in The United Arab Emirates. There are millions of unskilled expatriate laborers that earn a meager salary. Furthermore, The wealth gap in Abu Dhabi and Dubai has grown significantly in the last 10 to 15 years.

The government must take measures to ensure that those living below the poverty line have access to the technology that has become a necessity in today's world. This should be done by either moving workers out of labour camps and into areas of the city that have a high quality of infrastructure or by introducing technology in the labour camps. Many of these labourers would also need to learn how to use the devices as they have never gotten the chance to learn before.

Global Measures

In 2015, Physicist Stephen Hawking argues that technology is making inequality worse and that technological advances can leave most people "miserably poor."

This is why it is essential to keep the entire population up to date with technological advancements and not just the top 25th percentile which is the case in many countries.

O3B Networks, Ltd. is a worldwide network communications service provider. They have set a new goal of bridging the digital divide by making technology available for those people who cannot afford it both within The United States as well as in poorer countries abroad.

 

We have discussed how people are unable to benefit from technology due to the lack of access. On the other hand, there are people who simply do not have the skills needed to work an operating system or run a computer.

Age

Research has shown that there are more young people comfortable with modern technology than there are old people and this has allowed them to benefit more from the use of the internet. Older people may not be willing to buy a computer or not interested in using the internet.

This is also the reason that Families with children spend more money on setting up computers and the internet and on online purchases than families without children.

Gender

On a daily basis, Boys use computers and are commected to the internet for longer than girls. This may be due to the higher level of interest that boys have in online entertainment such as video games and streaming services


Education

As mentioned in the economic factors tab, to benefit from digital technology, one must have a certain degree of computer literacy. In the modern age, ICT is a subject that is being taught in most schools in developed and developing countries. However, the older population did not have the opportunity to learn these skills when they attended school and hence, were not able to benefit from the digital age. To obtain basic ICT Skills, a person must first be able to read and write. If they are not able to do so, then using the internet for entertainment purposes is likely tobe the extent of their ICT skills.

Local Measures

UAE Educator’s have said that technology is the key to good education in modern classrooms. The UAE Ministry of Education has announced the opening of its first technology center to connect students in different emirates.

In 2013, The UAE government made a huge investment in education (More that one-fifth of the total national budget) which included 14,000 iPads for universities. The UAE is also trying to minimize spending on school textbooks and looking to switch to technological gadgets which they believe is a more viable alternative.

Global Measures

In The United Kingdom, there are a number of initiatives being taken by the government seeking to narrow the digital divide. To begin with, schools and colleges have been funded and awarded appropriate grants so that they are able to purchase and make use of networking equipment. This will allow all students to learn basic IT skills. The government is also considering renting laptops and PCs to pupils.

In The UK, people who weren’t given the opportunity to learn computer skills in school can learn them through special websites being made which will have useful resources available that can help with self-learning.
There is a high amount of competition between communication companies.
The government hopes this will ensure a fast growth in Communications technology while reducing the price.

The government is also giving subsidies and tax holidays to businesses persuading them to use e-commerce and extend their markets. This will allow more people and businesses to become connected from different countries.
